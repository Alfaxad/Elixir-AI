Findings

Cancer has become a major threat in Africa, with a 45% increase of deaths since 2000, and it now kills half a million people each year, and even worse breast cancer & cervical cancer alone now cause 1 out of 5 cancer deaths in subsaharan Africa.

In Tanzania alone cancer covers more than 10% of the top 50 most causes of deaths, This is mainly due to poor screening methods and late-stage cancer detection, which leads to high cost for treatment and risk of death to a patient, not only in Tanzania but in most parts of Africa also, there is a huge disparity between Doctor to patient ratio, and there is low awareness of symptoms and diagnosis of cancer in most communities which lead cancer to be among the major health threats in the African continent.

About Elixir

Elixir intends to reduce the cancer threat in the world, focusing mostly in Tanzania and Africa in general. The Elixir web app offers a deep-learning approach (artificial intelligence) to the cancer detection and diagnosis. Using an integrated Artificial-Neural-Network model trained with approximately 200,000 microscopic images, Elixir provides a real-time cancer detection through microscopic images. The Elixir team chose to use microscopic images instead of X-ray, since unlike x-ray images microscopic images have less blind-spots and enables early detection of cancerous cells if present.

We believe that through early cancer detection and diagnosis, by using our web app we can reach more people and test, provide results and assist doctors to reach out more patients and reduce the risks caused by cancer thus improving the health sector and the welfare of the people.
